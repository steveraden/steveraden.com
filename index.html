Hello, I'm Steve Raden

I am a software developer and consultant and can help your organization envision and develop applications and data systems. I bring experience in

  • enterprise consulting
  • non-profit organizations
  • software development

enterprise consulting

Consulting has grounded me in the habit of considering the strategy or "Why?" of what an project or task means to the organization no matter how technical that action may be. It's also been invaluable to have exposed to the different problem solving styles of contributors and workers, managers and leaders.

non-profit organizations

Especially challenging and rewarding is the work I've done for non-profit and grassroots organizations both in the USA and abroad. Having far fewer resources than for-profit enterprises really motivates an organization to be selective and judicious in their technology choices. Consulting in this context can often involving empowering an organization through knowledge transfer of emerging techniques and trends from the business sector.

software development

I'm a senior developer whose worked on Python, Ruby on Rails and Node.JS SaaS projects and companies. In the past, I've been worked on some large .NET and Java code-bases, and also content management system hosted development such as Wordpress and Drupal. Though I've worked on the full stack, and pure front-end, the focus of my work has been on database programming, and SQL is my strongest language.

data engineering

My entry into the computer systems field was relational database technology. Many roles brought me adjacent to data services, analytics and database administration teams often because I was maintaining a data integration workflow which was non-performant and needed re-design around data engineering techniques. I'm training now on modern data engineering tool-sets such as Airflow, dbt and Google Cloud Platform Big Query.

systems administration

I've acted as web master for several non-profit organizations, and then went on to build out infrastructure on Amazon Web Services for several clients. Being able to translate between engineers and other staff is key during a crisis, your learn a lot keeping the lights on a system! However, the system's environment should really try to minimize heroism.

architecting and systems design

I enjoy listening about what an organization does and translating that into feature specifications and database design. I also like understanding the general ecology of organization's applications and technical infrastructure. And also how all that interacts with the ecology of the organization itself; the appropriateness and sustainability of technology choices is a really important consideration.
